What is Shell Shock?

It’s a psychological disturbance caused by prolonged exposure to active warfare, especially being under bombardment.This produced a helplessness appearing variously as panic and being scared, flight, or an inability to reason, sleep, walk or talk.

Symtoms

Symptoms included fatigue, tremor, confusion, nightmares and impaired sight and hearing. It was often diagnosed when a soldier was unable to function and no obvious cause could be identified.
